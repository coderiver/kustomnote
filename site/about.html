Our story

When Hussein Ahmed started tinkering with Evernote in 2012, he saw potential to do something big. A way to push it beyond a note-taking platform into something more robust. Something anyone could use to not just take notes, but organize their entire world.

He entered his idea (then called KustomNote) in Evernote’s DevCup competition, and was one of 10 finalists. After moving on to the ETC conference in San Francisco, the application won again--this time the Silver Cup and $15,000.

By now, Hussein knew he was on to something. He quit his day job at Amazon and began Transpose in earnest.
